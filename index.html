Chapter: Light - Reflection and Refraction

Introduction to Light:
- Light is a form of energy that enables us to see objects.
- It travels in straight lines and exhibits both wave and particle properties.

Reflection of Light:
- Reflection is the bouncing back of light when it strikes a surface.
- The law of reflection states that the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.
- Angle of Incidence (i) = Angle of Reflection (r)

Plane Mirror:
- Image formed in a plane mirror is virtual, erect, and of the same size as the object.
- Characteristics of the image formed by a plane mirror.

Spherical Mirrors:
- Concave and convex mirrors.
- Focal point, focal length, and center of curvature.

- Mirror formula: 1 f = 1 v + 1 u

- Magnification formula: m = - v u

Refraction of Light:
- Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.

- Refractive index: n = Speed of light in vacuum Speed of light in medium

Laws of Refraction:
- Snell's Law: n 1 sinθ 1 = n 2 sinθ 2
- Explanation of the laws and their applications.

Refraction by Spherical Lenses:
- Convex and concave lenses.
- Focal point, focal length, and optical center.

- Lens formula: 1 f = 1 v - 1 u

- Magnification formula: m = - v u

Power of a Lens:
- Power (P) of a lens: P = 1 f (measured in diopters,D).

Image Formation by Lenses:
- Characteristics of the image formed by convex and concave lenses.

Human Eye:
- Structure of the human eye.
- Accommodation and its defects: myopia and hypermetropia.

Defects of Vision:
- Correction of vision defects using lenses.

Practical Applications:
- Optical instruments like microscopes, telescopes, and cameras.
